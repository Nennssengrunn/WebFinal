◆◆ 01 — About the Event

Event Introduction

The National Polytechnic Institute of Cambodia (NPIC) is excited to host a hands on event featuring QO-100 (Es'hail-2), the world’s first geostationary satellite built for amateur radio. This is a unique opportunity for students and tech enthusiasts to connect with the stars right from our campus. Because we are located at the very edge of the satellite's reach, we will be working with a challenging 2degree elevation angle a feat that tests the limits of RF engineering. You will learn to transmit and receive live signals through a spacecraft orbiting 35,786 km above the Earth, gaining practical experience in antenna alignment and digital communication. Whether you are interested in hardware design or just curious about space, this event offers a genuine gateway to the cosmos.
